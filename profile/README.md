# GeneQuest

**Fibrous dysplasia first. Treatment path second. Open tools along the way.**

[GeneQuest Foundation](https://genequest.org) is a Polish non-profit (KRS 0001211461). We started here because our family lives **fibrous dysplasia / McCune-Albright syndrome (FD/MAS)** — and we are building the network, knowledge, and research infrastructure to move from diagnosis toward **better treatment**, not just better information.

If we find a path that works for FD, we will help other rare-disease families walk a similar one. The architecture is designed to extend; the urgency is personal.

---

## Why we exist

In 2023 our son's diagnostic journey went through histopathology, conflicting opinions, and a treatment plan that pointed toward major surgery. A **GNAS genetic test** — ordered on our own initiative — corrected the diagnosis to FD. Expert clinicians in Poland, Italy, and the Netherlands confirmed that **observation, not resection**, was the right course for a child without pain or functional impairment.

That path should not depend on luck, PubMed mining at 2 a.m., or knowing the right person in Olsztyn. We build GeneQuest so the next family has a map — and so we can reach the clinicians and researchers who can move FD therapy forward.

> Rare diseases have a quiet weight. You don't build a monument to that — you build tools, community, and relationships with people who actually know the disease.

## What we're doing now

| Track | What | Why it matters |
|-------|------|----------------|
| **Families & clinicians** | [genequest.org](https://genequest.org) — FD/MAS portal in PL + EN | GNAS after suspicious histopathology. Surgery is not the default in children. Doctor database. Guidelines you can print and bring to an appointment. |
| **Living guidelines** | [GeneGuidelines](https://github.com/GeneQuestOrg/GeneGuidelines) · [demo](https://geneguidelines.genequest.org) | PubMed watcher + clinician-reviewed updates. Parent decision trees + doctor review workflow. Complements [Orphanet](https://www.orpha.net) — we link to ground truth and add a living layer. |
| **Research** | Research Canvas *(in development)* | Agentic workflows for rare-disease research: deterministic pipeline skeleton, AI blocks inside, full audit trail. First use case: **drug target prioritization for FD**. |

We use AI seriously — not as a pitch deck word. One founder with domain knowledge and good AI tooling can ship what used to require a lab plus a dev shop. That productivity is how we turn contacts into collaborations and collaborations into hypotheses worth testing.

## Research directions we care about

These are hypotheses, not promises — but they shape what we build and who we talk to:

- Targeted approaches that block **aberrant GNAS signaling** without hitting healthy cells — a direction actively explored by leading bone-biology groups at major research universities
- **Drug target prioritization** from literature + structured evidence, not ad-hoc reading lists
- Models closer to human biology than mouse-only shortcuts (organoids, iPSC-derived systems)
- Faster publication of **case studies and preprints** when the standard journal cycle is too slow for families making decisions now
